Multi-sensor fusion for GNSS-denied navigation

LOG 01 · NavINST — RMC / Queen's

When GPS fails — urban canyons, RF interference — a vehicle still needs to know its heading and attitude. My research fuses unconventional sensing (polarization and event cameras) with inertial navigation to keep the state estimate alive when conventional signals degrade.

I develop the ML side of the pipeline: compute-constrained classifiers (SVM, logistic regression, SGD) for real-time solar meridian detection from polarization imagery — absolute heading with no magnetometer and no GNSS — plus CNN models for visual navigation cues, all fused with the IMU through an EKF and evaluated against survey-grade NovAtel ground truth on a vehicle-mounted rig.

NOW: extending the stack toward closed-loop testing on a Clearpath Husky UGV and autonomous UAV platforms.

Attitude RMSE
0.87° yaw · 0.05° roll/pitch
Sensors
Polarization cam · event cam · RGB · Xsens IMU · NovAtel GNSS (reference)
Methods
EKF fusion · SVM / LR / SGD · CNN · simulation of degraded-GNSS scenarios

Robotic end effector for the Franka Emika Panda

LOG 02 · Capstone

Designed and built a low-cost three-finger gripper for the Franka Emika Panda arm — 3D-printed in carbon-fiber nylon, mechanical design validated with SolidWorks FEA, and driven by an Arduino control stack (I2C, PCA9685 PWM) with DS5160 servos and FSR402 force-sensitive resistors closing the loop on grip force so it can hold fragile objects and safely high-five a human.

The result outperformed its spec: rated for 2 kg payloads, it held 7.2 kg in testing, closing in 0.39 s, at a total build cost of ~$300 — versus ~$2,550 for the commercial gripper. Alongside it I prototyped an OpenCV + MediaPipe hand-tracking layer as the path toward gesture-based control.

Control

Bio-inspired polarization navigation

LOG 03 · Research

Desert ants navigate by reading the polarization pattern of the sky. This project borrows that trick: a polarization camera and lightweight ML extract the solar meridian to recover absolute heading — a compass that works precisely where magnetometers and GNSS don't.

The emphasis is efficiency: the detection pipeline is built from low-compute classifiers so it can run in real time on modest embedded hardware rather than a GPU.

Principle
Skylight polarization → solar meridian → absolute heading
Compute
Real-time on embedded-class hardware

IoBIO — plant-AI hardware platform

LOG 04 · Lead Electrical Engineer

As lead electrical engineer at a plant-AI startup, I took the sensing hardware from schematic to shipped: custom PCB design, ESP32 firmware with on-device DSP for plant biosignals, and the IoT plumbing to move data reliably from greenhouse to cloud.

The V2 board packs the full sensing chain into 30 × 68 mm: an ESP32‑S3‑WROOM‑1 running the show, an ADS1115 16‑bit ADC on a dedicated low‑noise LDO rail (TPS7A2033) for the microvolt‑level plant biosignals, BME280 environmental and VEML7700 ambient‑light sensors, USB‑C with USBLC6 ESD protection, and a WS2812B status LED — analog and digital domains kept deliberately apart across the two layers.
